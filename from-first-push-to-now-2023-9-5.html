Building Loom

From first push to now | 2023-9-5
Starting July 2023 I decided to take up a challenge on building an entire client that can support content aggregation. I had discovered a stack that had been built to decentralize a format of consumption similar to Reddit. Incorporating a networking structure known as Federation. Similar to how email works, a user can sign-up on any service that provides a certain set of protocols of communication to connect with users under other domains.
Similar to the NFT craze of 2021, we saw another experience similar to this. When those owning NFT tokens could use extensions such as Metamask to verify ownership of a particular token, then certain priveleges are granted. Such as, being able to customize a particular facet of a feature that was locked prior. This was a special UX to me, as it felt like a unique metaphor of a key-value relationship or user/password. Even though we still needed a user/password to access metamask. Overall, being crpyto related, the nature of transparency added other foundational values that strengthened this concept into a movement that was dubbed “Web3”. But, the caveat is it wanted to keep “members” within a closed community per token. The similarity I found, was this need to change the medium of what defines an online identity and who sets the rules of how others see this identity. And, having a single wallet own many NFTs or “Accounts” to access unique experiences respectively.
There are still many questions left un-answered to “Web3” motives and/or process of executing this unique online verification. But, federation incorporates a classical approach with a greater focus on communicating with those outside of the “circle”. As in, your opinions, thoughts are not closed and controlled within a centralized site/corporation. They can federate into the lands of other social domains that choose to federate. Sort of seems similar to ERC-6551 that is currently being reviewed, if implemented by developers in the same respect as ActivityPub.
Transparent credentials, secured by you, communicating in a transparent social network owned by none. This seems like an interesting experiment.
Loom started off as an experiment too. I was fascinated when I spun-up a Lemmy instance so easily, early July. It felt like I was making my own personal Reddit. My own space where I could divide particular interests of mine into sub-categories for other federated servers to see, pick, and choose to align with theirs. I moved on to wanting to build an iOS application to facilitate my content creation needs. Evolving, to how I wanted to change the way I browsed the content. Evolving to wanting to mature Granite further to support the architecture of federation/social networks. And eventually the need to aggregate all types of content for consumption, with the rules I set. But, making sure these lines are not hard-coded and are customizable by others.
I’ve felt all these sites like Twitter, Reddit, etc. Are nothing but re-skins of the same data structure to display content. Why not just have a singular application that can allow you to well, re-skin, the “feed” to look like any of them. Implement a way to communicate with all of them or simply browse them. And then share them or facilitate discussion.
That’s where Loom is now.
Mastodon, Kbin, Lemmy are all providing great solutions to jump-start self-hosting needs in a very vibrant and passionate open-source community. The communities being varied in beliefs and views, but seem to remain strong in their commitments to maintain authentic solutions to an open social network. Even though this has been around for quite some time, it took me a while to connect the dots or see the importance of this open nature.
I’ve spent the past 4-5 years building closed source packages on my own time, not feeling worthy of sharing or giving-back even. As most of my learnings have come from self-taught exploration around open-source projects. The basis of my motivations is rooted in mobile development and rendering technologies, I’ve simply just pushed code privately and released random abstract thoughts onto the App Stores and other sites with no linear “story”. Finally, open-sourcing and focusing on actual utility this year has formed a new set of engineering principles within. Still calibrating of-course. But, being able to slowly tie all of my work together into a singular thought, Loom, has finally given me direction.
